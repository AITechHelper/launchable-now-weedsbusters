FAQ

Questions We Hear All the Time

Still on the fence? Here are answers to the most common questions homeowners ask before calling us.

Do I have to sign a contract? ▾
Absolutely not. We don't believe in locking people in. You can schedule one treatment or ten — it's entirely up to you. We'd rather earn your repeat business by doing a great job than trap you in a contract.
Is it safe for my kids and pets? ▾
Yes. Once our treatments have dried — typically 1–2 hours after application — your lawn is completely safe for kids and pets to enjoy. We'll let you know the recommended re-entry window after every visit.
How soon will I see results? ▾
Post-emergent treatments typically show visible results within 7–14 days as existing weeds die off. Pre-emergent treatments work underground to prevent new weeds from sprouting — you'll notice fewer weeds appearing over the following weeks.
How much does weed control cost? ▾
Every lawn is different, so we price based on your lot size and the level of weed pressure we're dealing with. We build plans around your budget and will never recommend a service you don't need. Call or text for a same-day quote.
What types of weeds do you treat? ▾
We treat all common Oklahoma weeds including crabgrass, dandelions, clover, nutsedge, spurge, and more. We identify exactly what's in your lawn before applying any treatment to make sure we're using the right product.
Do you service commercial properties? ▾
Yes! We work with both residential and commercial properties throughout the Tulsa metro. Whether it's a single-family home or a commercial lot, we can put together a maintenance plan that keeps it looking sharp year-round.
What if the weeds come back? ▾
If weeds return between scheduled treatments, just give us a call. We stand behind our work and will come back to address any breakthrough growth — that's part of our satisfaction commitment to every customer.
When is the best time to start? ▾
Honestly? Right now. The best time to get ahead of weeds is before they're fully established. Spring is peak season, but we provide effective treatments throughout the year. The sooner we start, the better your lawn will look this season.
